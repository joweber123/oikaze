In a nutshell

The Tohoku region of Japan shines with every season. Fall explodes with the burnt orange and blood red of maples. Spring paints the hills and wind pink with falling “sakura” petals. Anytime of the year is great for viewing Matsushima Bay with its over 200 pine tree covered islands. The Sanriku coast twists in and out while huge cliffs offer panoramic views of the rugged coastline. Perhaps you prefer virgin old-growth forests, in which case the Shirakami Sanchi region is the perfect place to explore. We’ll ride up quiet mountain paths (often unpaved) and put on our hiking shoes once the road ends at the trailhead. Lunch at the waterfall? Yes, please. Apart from natural beauty, this region lights up every summer for “Tohoku Sandai Matsuri”, the three great festivals of Tohoku. Aomori’s Nebuta Matsuri, Akita's Kanto Matsuri and Sendai's Tanabata are more than enough reason to come to this part of the world during the summertime. Drummers and musicians usher along enormous Nebuta floats that this region’s artists spend nearly all year making. If you’re looking to get away from the crowds lets head north to the mountains of Tohoku!

Best times to come

December, January, February is pretty cold and wet in tohoku and not the most ideal time for cycling. We would recommend stearing clear of January and February in this part of the country if you are looking to be outside a lot.

March, April, May is a great time in this part of the country and a splendid time to come. You will get some cherry blossoms if visiting in April, but just about every week there is something new blooming in this part of the country.

June is when the rainy season starts and you are best to avoid cycling in a typhoon if at all possible.

July, August is really really hot and humid and we wouldn't recommend getting on a bicycle for any amount of time during the day during this time of the year.

September, October, November is one of the most beautiful times to come to tohoku. The leaves are changing bright red, orange and yellow and the scenery really comes alive.
