JAPAN SEA

ニホンカイ

In a nutshell

The iconic countryside of Japan sea region bustles along quietly, just out of reach of the normal tourist itinerary. The towering northern alps to the east have kept this region to its own devices throughout Japan’s history and allowed those willing to put up with some rain and snow to indulge in some of the countries most stunning vistas. Kanazawa is spectacular and a microcosm of all that is good in Japanese culture. Whether you are strolling down one of the best-preserved Edo-period districts in the country or meandering through the breathtaking Kenroku-en garden, you can rest assured you are witnessing Japan at its highest. Outside the cities the terrain is rugged and many spectacular examples of traditional Japanese farm architecture dot the landscape. On your way visit timeless sake breweries and see where the best rice in Japan turns into the best sake. Follow the coast and enjoy views that have inspired Japanese poets and writers for centuries. Go into the mountains to find hidden onsen and untouched wilderness. No matter where we choose to go, this region offers a wonderful mix of well worth the trip cultural cities and off the grid wilderness.

Best times to come

December, January, February is pretty cold and wet on the Japan-sea and not the most ideal time for cycling. We would recommend stearing clear of January and February in this part of the country if you are looking to be outside a lot.

March, April, May is a great time in this part of the country and a splendid time to come. You will get some cherry blossoms if visiting in April, but just about every week there is something new blooming in this part of the country.

June is when the rainy season starts and you are best to avoid cycling in a typhoon if at all possible.

July, August is really really hot and humid and we wouldn't recommend getting on a bicycle for any amount of time during the day during this time of the year.

September, October, November is one of the most beautiful times to come to the Japan-sea. The leaves are changing bright red, orange and yellow and the scenery really comes alive.
