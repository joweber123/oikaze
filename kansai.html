カンサイ

In a nutshell

The region of Kansai is undoubtedly the cultural heart of Japan. This is where even Japanese people go to experience the wonders of Japanese culture. Kyoto has 17 world heritage properties and over 2000 temples and shrines sprinkled across the city and boost the highest Michelin star restaurants per capita of anywhere on the planet. Nearby Nara is where you will find the world's largest wooden building housing an enormous 15 meter tall buddha just down the street from the world's oldest wooden building. Wakayama’s Koya-san is the spiritual center of the region and one of Japan’s most powerful holy sites. Outside of these obvious and internationally known locales other Japan biggest and oldest include Saki-no-Yu Onsen, said to be the oldest onsen in Japan, along with Nachi Waterfall, the largest waterfall in Japan. It is easy to see why this region is a must see for many who want to see Japan at its most historic, most delicious and most refined. After soaking in all the natural wonders of Kansai its time to soak up some street food and a local craft beer in Osaka. Being the 2nd/3rd largest city in Japan depending on time of day (oddly true) Osaka has much to offer for those looking to try Japan’s wild side. While Tokyo is the place with overwhelming views, Osaka is the place to make wild friends and drinking buddies. Unlike polite and quiet Tokyo, Osaka people are more likely to share the space and time with strangers without feeling like an interruption. Kansai has it all, from tiny side streets in the big city to quiet shrines among ancient trees this is one of our favorite places to explore by bicycle and on foot.

Best times to come

December, January, February is pretty cold and wet in kansai and not the most ideal time for cycling. We would recommend stearing clear of January and February in this part of the country if you are looking to be outside a lot.

March, April, May is a great time in this part of the country and a splendid time to come. You will get some cherry blossoms if visiting in April, but just about every week there is something new blooming in this part of the country.

June is when the rainy season starts and you are best to avoid cycling in a typhoon if at all possible.

July, August is really really hot and humid and we wouldn't recommend getting on a bicycle for any amount of time during the day during this time of the year.

September, October, November is one of the most beautiful times to come to kansai. The leaves are changing bright red, orange and yellow and the scenery really comes alive.
